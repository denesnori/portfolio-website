About me

Here is a brief story, who I am, what I used to do, what I am up to now.

My background is in Chemistry, but over time, my interest slowly shifted towards Theoretical and Computational Chemistry, and in the end to programming. The final my blow came when I moved to London from Hungary. I saw it as a best possible opportunity for a fresh start. I volunteered at conferences, and attended tons of meetups to learn more about my options and found my new passion in web development.

Now I am a full-stack JavaScript web developer living in London, currently learning and freelancing at Founders & Coders.

I enjoy creating websites that people use. Coming from an academic background, I take huge pleasure in working together with people from diverse backgrounds. I am an avid learner, I enjoy that every day there is something new and exciting to play around with.

There are plenty of ways to reach me listed in the contacts section.

About me

What I am up to If not coding?

Meetups

I love the meetups. I can't keep track of the number of meetup groups I signed up for. I have already met many highly motivated people changing careers, wanting to do something good or just improve their knowledge.

Code Your Future

I have recently started volunteering at Code Your Future, a coding school for refugees. They teach CSS/HTML, Javascript and backend technologies. I help them to deliver their backend and database module.

Volunteering

Coming from a non-Computer Science background, volunteering for coding conferences gives me an amzing opportunity to broaden my horizon, forge new friendships and give something back to the developer community. Last year I volunteered for DevoxxUK and ODSC London.

Hiking

I fell in love with hiking when I walked the El Camino ( the French Way) in 2014. Hiking is an amazing way of exploring nature and getting away from our busy everyday life. Now my goal is to walk as many of the Scottish long distance hiking trails as I can.

Tennis

I love playing and watching tennis. I even had a taste of being a chair umpire. when I volunteered at junior tennis tournaments. One of the great gifts of living in London was to be able to attend the Wimbledon tennis tournament last year. It was a dream come true!
